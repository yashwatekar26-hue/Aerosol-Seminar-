Aerosol Jet Printing

Three Papers, One Mission

Seminar Presentation · 2025

02 / 11

What is Aerosol Jet Printing?

  • Contactless printing – head hovers 1–5 mm above surface
  • Sprays functional materials (silver, ceramics, polymers)
  • Prints lines as thin as 10 µm – thinner than a hair!
  • Can print on curved or uneven surfaces
  • Two mist creation methods: ultrasonic (uniform) & pneumatic (faster)
03 / 11

📄 Paper 1 – Wilkinson & Smith (2019)

Review Paper – The Foundation

  • Summarises everything known about AJP up to 2018
  • Explains physics – droplet formation, focusing, materials
  • Works with many materials – silver, ceramics, even proteins
  • Limitations: resolution limited to ~10 µm, overspray problem
⭐ 4 / 5 – Best foundation
04 / 11

📄 Paper 2 – Hwang & Choi (2024)

Mid‑Air Blending – The Mixing Solution

  • Problem: nanoparticles clump when mixed before printing
  • Solution: two separate atomizers – combine only at the printhead
  • Result: perfect dispersion, no clumps
  • Performance: dielectric constant 14 → 28 (double!)
  • 16× better electrical properties for multi‑layer structures
⭐ 4.5 / 5 – Solves blending
05 / 11

📄 Paper 3 – Jung & Choi (2019)

Electric‑Field 3D Printing – The Resolution Champion

  • Uses electric fields to guide charged nanoparticles
  • Prints true 3D structures – pillars, spirals, the letters “3D”
  • Resolution < 100 nm – 100× smaller than conventional AJP
  • Can switch between different metals during printing
  • Catch: only works with single metals – no blending
⭐ 5 / 5 – Visionary concept
